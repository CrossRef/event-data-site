About Crossref Event Data

Much of the web activity around scholarly content happens outside of the formal literature. The scholarly community needs an infrastructure that collects, stores, and openly makes available these interactions. Thatâ€™s why we at Crossref are working to develop a new service that will provide a means of monitoring and displaying links to scholarly content on the open web. Our belief is that the greater visibility provided by Crossref Event Data will help publishers, authors, bibliometricians and libraries to develop a fuller understanding of where and how scholarly content is being shared and consumed.

How it will work

Crossref Event Data collects a wide variety of events related to any content with a DOI. These events may include bookmarks, comments, social shares, citations, and links to other research entities. The Crossref Events Data service identifies and stores these events, and makes the data openly available so that anyone can see how the content is being shared. Importantly, publishers and vendors will be able to process and interpret the data for their own services.
